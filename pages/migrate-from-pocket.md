<p class="contentLead">If you already have your Pocket export, you can import it into wallabag and keep reading with an open source read-it-later app.</p>

Pocket shut down in 2025 and exporting data from the service is no longer available. This guide assumes you already downloaded your Pocket export while it was still possible.

wallabag can be used in two ways: you can self-host your own instance, or use [wallabag.it](https://www.wallabag.it/en) if you prefer a hosted service with upgrades and backups handled for you.

## Pocket migration checklist

1. Locate the Pocket CSV export you already downloaded and keep the original file somewhere safe.
2. Create or choose a wallabag instance: [self-host wallabag](/self-hosting/), use [wallabag.it](https://www.wallabag.it/en), or look for a community host such as [CHATONS](https://www.chatons.org/taxonomy/term/317).
3. Import your Pocket CSV export into wallabag.
4. Review the imported entries and keep the original export as a backup.
5. Install the browser extension or mobile app you want to use for saving new articles.

## What to expect

The import is designed to bring your saved Pocket links into wallabag. Once imported, wallabag can fetch article content, let you tag entries, archive what you have read and keep a searchable reading list.

Some pages may be unavailable, blocked, deleted or difficult to extract depending on the original website. Since Pocket exports can no longer be requested, keeping your original export is especially important.
